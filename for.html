Who Qualifies

The NLV Is Designed for People Who Will Not Work in Spain

Spain's Non-Lucrative Visa is specifically for non-EU nationals who want to live in Spain long term without working. The core requirement is proving you have enough passive income — approximately €2,400/month for a single applicant — to support yourself without relying on employment in Spain.

The NLV is right for you if you have:

  • State pension (UK State Pension, US Social Security, Australian Age Pension, etc.)
  • Private or occupational pension income
  • Investment dividends or ETF income
  • Rental income from property you own
  • Savings above ~€28,800 (single applicant) to supplement income
  • A combination of the above sources meeting ~€2,400/month total

The NLV is NOT right for you if you:

  • Plan to work remotely for a non-Spanish employer or client (→ Digital Nomad Visa)
  • Have €500,000+ to invest in Spanish real estate (→ Golden Visa)
  • Have passive income below ~€760/month and prefer a lower threshold (→ Portugal D7)
  • Need to continue working in any capacity to fund your stay in Spain

Key Facts

What All NLV Applicants Need to Know

💶

Income threshold

Approximately €2,400/month for a single applicant (400% of Spain's IPREM), updated each January. Each additional family member adds approximately €600/month. The threshold applies to your combined household, not each individual.

🏥

Health insurance required

You must have comprehensive private health insurance with no co-payments and no excess from a provider authorised to operate in Spain. NHS entitlement and travel insurance are not accepted. Costs range from approximately €500–€2,000/year per person.

📋

Criminal record certificate

All applicants aged 18 and over must provide an apostilled criminal record certificate from every country they have lived in for more than 6 months over the past 5 years. UK applicants need a DBS certificate; US applicants typically need an FBI background check.

🏠

Proof of accommodation

You need to show where you plan to live in Spain — either a rental contract or a property ownership certificate. You do not need to have moved yet, but you do need to have accommodation arranged before your consulate appointment.

⏱️

Processing time

Most consulates process NLV applications within 1–3 months. Some consulates are faster than others — London, New York, and Miami tend to be efficient. Our dashboard lets you track your application status at every stage.

🔄

Renewal & path to residency

The initial NLV lasts 1 year and can be renewed inside Spain for 2-year periods. After 5 years of continuous legal residence, you can apply for long-term EU residency. After 10 years, Spanish nationality may be available depending on your circumstances.

Common Questions

Who Can Get Spain's NLV — FAQ

Can retirees get Spain's Non-Lucrative Visa?

Yes — retirees are the most common NLV applicants. If you have a state pension, private pension, or a combination of pension and investment income totalling approximately €2,400/month, you can qualify. Both UK State Pension and US Social Security count as qualifying passive income. Our retirees guide covers the specific documents and evidence you need.

Can families apply for Spain's NLV together?

Yes — families can apply together with one lead applicant. Each additional family member increases the income threshold by approximately €600/month: a couple needs approximately €3,000/month, a couple with one child approximately €3,600/month, and so on. All family members are included in a single managed application. See our families guide and our family application service.

Can I use the NLV to take a sabbatical in Spain?

Yes — if you have sufficient passive income or savings (~€2,400/month or ~€28,800 in accessible savings for a single applicant), and you will not work during your stay. Sabbatical-takers often use a combination of savings, rental income from a UK or US property, and investment income. You must not work in Spain — including remote work for your current employer. Our sabbatical guide covers how to document this situation.

Is the NLV suitable for people following the FIRE movement?

Very much so. The NLV is arguably Spain's best route for FIRE adherents — it allows you to live in Spain full time on investment income, dividends, ETF returns, and savings drawdowns without working. The key is evidencing that your investment income is regular and sustainable over the visa period. Our FIRE guide explains the documentation approach in detail.

Can I apply for the NLV if I am still employed but planning to retire?

Timing is important here. The consulate assesses income you are currently receiving, not income you plan to receive in future. If you are still employed and your pension has not yet started, you cannot count future pension income now. However, if you have savings or investment income that currently meets the threshold, you may be able to apply now and retire to Spain before your pension starts. Our specialists advise on optimal timing for your specific situation.
