Spain NLV Appeal Timeline: The One-Month Deadline & Processing Time

Time is critical when your NLV is rejected. You have exactly one month from notification to lodge a formal appeal, or that right is gone forever. Understand the timeline, the deadline, and what to expect during the appeal process.

The One-Month Deadline (Critical)

Spanish administrative law gives you 30 days from the date of notification to lodge a formal appeal (recurso de alzada). This is not a guideline — it's a hard legal deadline. After 30 days, you cannot appeal. Your only option is to reapply.

How to Count the 30 Days

  • Start date: The date on your refusal letter (the date the consulate issued the decision)
  • Not: The date you received it (which may be days or weeks later)
  • End date: Exactly 30 days later

Example: Refusal letter dated April 1, 2026. Deadline is May 1, 2026, by end of business day. If May 1 is a weekend or holiday, the deadline may be extended to the next business day (varies by jurisdiction).

What "Lodging an Appeal" Means

Your appeal must be formally submitted to the consulate within the deadline. "Submitting" typically means:

  • Hand-delivered to the consulate (get a receipt)
  • Sent by registered mail (with proof of posting showing the date)
  • Submitted via the consulate's online system (if they have one)

Email is not sufficient. Use registered/tracked mail or in-person submission.

Full NLV Appeal Timeline

Days 1-3: Notification & Understanding Refusal

  • Receive refusal letter
  • Read the grounds for refusal carefully
  • Understand exactly why you were refused

Days 4-10: Decision — Appeal or Reapply?

  • Assess whether appealing makes sense
  • Gather evidence of what went wrong
  • Contact a specialist (if using one)

Days 11-25: Prepare Appeal Documentation

  • Collect new or clarifying evidence
  • Write detailed response to refusal grounds
  • Address each point in refusal letter
  • Prepare supporting documents
  • Have appeal translated into Spanish (if needed)

Days 26-30: Submit Appeal

  • Final review of appeal document
  • Submit via registered mail or in person
  • Get proof of submission (dated receipt, tracking number)
  • Keep copy for your records

Months 2-3: Consulate Reviews Appeal

  • Consulate receives and logs your appeal
  • They review the original refusal decision
  • They assess your new evidence
  • They determine whether to uphold, reverse, or request more info

Month 3-4: Decision Issued

  • Consulate issues decision on appeal
  • You receive approval, continued refusal, or request for additional info

Processing Time by Consulate

Appeal processing times vary widely by consulate:

  • Faster consulates (2-3 weeks decision): Madrid, Barcelona, smaller consulates with fewer applications
  • Average consulates (4-8 weeks decision): Most major consulates
  • Slower consulates (8-12 weeks decision): High-volume consulates (London, New York)

These are rough guides. Don't assume a timeline — contact your consulate to ask about typical appeal processing times.

Total Timeline: Rejection to Decision

  • Rejection to deadline: 1 month
  • Preparation time: 2-3 weeks
  • Consulate review time: 4-8 weeks
  • Total: 3-4 months (best case: 10 weeks; worst case: 5 months)

From initial application to final appeal decision can be 6-8 months or longer.

What Happens If You Miss the Deadline?

You cannot appeal. Missing the deadline is final.

Your only option is to reapply, starting from scratch. You lose the right to appeal the original decision.

How People Miss the Deadline

  • Refusal letter is delayed in postal system; by the time they receive it, deadline has passed
  • They miscount the 30 days
  • They assume the deadline is flexible
  • They wait for consulate office hours or try to submit near the end of day

Extending the Deadline (Unlikely)

Spanish administrative law is rigid on appeal deadlines. Extensions are not typically granted. You may be able to request an extension if:

  • You can prove you didn't receive notification (very difficult)
  • Extraordinary circumstances prevented you from appealing (war, severe illness — very high bar)

Don't count on this. Treat the 30-day deadline as absolute.

Timeline Tips

  • Act immediately upon rejection. Don't wait to understand the refusal — start the clock the day you receive it
  • Calculate your exact deadline in writing (e.g., "Refusal dated April 1, deadline May 1")
  • Set calendar reminders at Day 10, 20, and 25 — this ensures you don't accidentally miss the deadline
  • Assume consulate mail is slow. Don't send appeal via regular mail the last day; send on Day 25 at latest to ensure arrival
  • Keep proof of submission. Registered mail receipt or in-person submission receipt with timestamp
  • Follow up after 4 weeks. Contact consulate to confirm your appeal was received and logged

After Appeal Decision

  • If approved: Consulate will guide you next steps (visa issuance, appointment scheduling)
  • If rejected again: You can reapply immediately, or appeal the second decision if new grounds exist
  • If requested for more info: Deadline to submit will be specified in the request (typically 30 days)
