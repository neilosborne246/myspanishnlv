Practical Essentials

Key Steps When You Arrive in Spain on Your NLV

Your visa is approved — now what? Here are the essential administrative steps every NLV holder should complete after arriving in Spain.

🏢

Register on the Padrón

Register your address at your local town hall (Ayuntamiento) as soon as possible after arriving. The Padrón certificate is required for your TIE card application, school enrolment, and various Spanish administrative processes. It is free and straightforward — bring your passport, visa, and proof of address. See our Empadronamiento guide.

🪪

Apply for Your NIE & TIE Card

Your NIE (Número de Identificación de Extranjero) is your Spanish tax identification number — you need it for almost everything in Spain. Your TIE (Tarjeta de Identidad de Extranjero) is your physical residency card. Both must be obtained after arriving in Spain. Our specialists can guide you through the process. See our NIE/TIE guide.

🏦

Open a Spanish Bank Account

A Spanish bank account makes day-to-day life significantly easier — direct debits for utilities, rent payments, and local transactions. Many banks are foreigner-friendly and English-speaking. Having your NIE is typically required to open an account. Some NLV holders open an account before arriving through international-friendly banks like Bankinter or BBVA.

📋

Understand Your Tax Position

If you spend more than 183 days per year in Spain, you become a Spanish tax resident and must file a Spanish IRPF tax return declaring your worldwide income. Spain has double taxation treaties with the UK, USA, Ireland, Australia, Canada, and New Zealand — preventing double taxation on the same income. See our Tax hub for a full overview.

Common Questions

Life in Spain on the NLV — FAQ

What is the best city to live in Spain on the NLV?

There is no single best city — it depends on your lifestyle and priorities. Valencia offers excellent value: Mediterranean climate, high-quality life, good international connections, and significantly lower costs than Madrid or Barcelona. Málaga is popular for its warm climate and growing English-speaking community. Alicante and the Costa Blanca attract many British and Irish retirees for the established expat networks and affordable property. Our full Best Cities guide covers each region in detail.

How much does it cost to live in Spain on the NLV?

A couple living comfortably outside Madrid or Barcelona typically spends €2,000–€3,500/month all-in (rent, food, utilities, health insurance, transport, and leisure). In smaller cities and coastal towns, comfortable living is achievable on €2,000–€2,500/month for a couple. Spain remains significantly cheaper than the UK, Switzerland, or the US for a comparable lifestyle. See our full Cost of Living guide.

Can I access Spanish public healthcare as an NLV holder?

NLV holders must have private health insurance as a visa condition. You are not automatically entitled to Spanish public healthcare. However, some regions allow long-term foreign residents who are Padrón-registered to access the public system for a fee. In practice, most NLV holders use their private insurance throughout their stay — Spain's private healthcare is excellent and the cost is generally affordable. Our Healthcare guide covers your options in full.

Do I need to speak Spanish to live in Spain?

In most expat-heavy areas — the Costa Blanca, Costa del Sol, Valencia, Málaga, Alicante — English is widely spoken and many English-speakers live very comfortably with minimal Spanish. However, some Spanish makes bureaucratic interactions (Padrón registration, NIE appointments, dealing with landlords) much smoother. If you plan to live in a smaller village or rural area, some Spanish becomes more important. Most NLV holders pick up functional Spanish within the first year simply through daily life.

What is the Padrón and do I have to register?

Yes — the Padrón Municipal de Habitantes is Spain's local population register and NLV holders are required to register at their local Ayuntamiento (town hall). Registration unlocks access to local services, is required for your TIE card, for school enrolment, and for many official processes. It is free and typically straightforward. Bring your passport, visa sticker or entry stamp, and proof of your Spanish address. Our Empadronamiento guide has full instructions.
