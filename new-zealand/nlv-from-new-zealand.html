Non-Lucrative Visa from New Zealand — The Kiwi Guide to Moving to Spain

New Zealand citizens can apply for Spain's Non-Lucrative Visa through the Spanish Embassy in Wellington. This complete guide covers NZ Police Vetting, MFAT apostille, income requirements in NZD, and everything you need to know about applying as a Kiwi.

Eligibility

Can New Zealanders Get Spain's Non-Lucrative Visa?

Yes — absolutely. As New Zealand is not an EU or EEA member state, Kiwi citizens need a visa to reside in Spain beyond the 90-day visa-free tourist allowance. The Non-Lucrative Visa from New Zealand is the primary route for New Zealanders who want to live in Spain without working for a Spanish employer — whether that's for retirement, a lifestyle change, or simply to enjoy everything Spain has to offer.

The NLV allows you to reside in Spain for one year initially, with the option to renew for 2+2 further years. You do not need to be a certain age, and you do not need to be retired — you simply need to demonstrate that you can support yourself financially without working in Spain.

Key Facts for NZ Applicants

Consulate: Spanish Embassy, 56 Victoria Street, Wellington 6011

Police certificate: NZ Police Vetting Service certificate

Apostille authority: Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Trade (MFAT)

Income threshold: ~€2,400/month (~NZD $4,300/month)

NZ Super eligibility: Qualifying income source, but usually needs topping up

Application service fee: €1,499 total

Financial Requirements

Income Requirements for Kiwi Applicants

The core income threshold for Spain's NLV is approximately €2,400 per month (400% of Spain's IPREM indicator) for a single applicant. In New Zealand dollars, at 2026 exchange rates, this equates to approximately NZD $4,200–4,500 per month — though this figure fluctuates with the NZD/EUR exchange rate, so always check the current ECB rate when calculating.

Qualifying Income Sources for New Zealanders

NZ Superannuation

Government pension from age 65. In 2026, a single person receives approximately NZD $3,000/month (~€1,650/month). This is typically below the €2,400 threshold and needs supplementing with KiwiSaver or investment income.

KiwiSaver Drawdowns

Regular withdrawals from KiwiSaver (once you have reached the qualifying age) are accepted as passive income. Consistent monthly withdrawals documented over 3–6 months work well as evidence.

NZ Property Rental Income

Rental income from New Zealand property qualifies as passive income. Provide tenancy agreements and bank statements showing regular deposits. Overseas (NZ) rental income is a common and well-accepted income source.

NZ Investment Income

Dividends from NZ shares, managed fund distributions, or interest from term deposits all qualify. Provide brokerage statements or bank records showing regular income over the past 3–6 months.

NZ Super shortfall: NZ Superannuation alone is typically around €750/month short of the NLV threshold. The most common solution is combining NZ Super with KiwiSaver drawdowns or investment portfolio income. Our specialists will help you structure your income evidence to meet the threshold.

Criminal Record Certificate

The New Zealand Police Vetting Certificate

Every NLV applicant must provide a criminal record certificate from their country of residence, apostilled for use in Spain. For New Zealand applicants, this is the NZ Police Vetting Service certificate — also known as a Criminal History Report.

Step 1: Obtain Your NZ Police Vetting Certificate

Apply through the NZ Police website (police.govt.nz) under the "Vetting" section, or use a licensed third-party vetting service. You will need to provide proof of identity. The standard certificate covers all disclosable conviction history.

Cost: Approximately NZD $10–15 for the standard certificate.

Processing time: Typically 2–4 weeks from application to receipt.

Step 2: MFAT Apostille

Once you have your Police Vetting certificate, it must be apostilled by the New Zealand Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Trade (MFAT). New Zealand is a Hague Apostille Convention signatory, so the process is straightforward.

Cost: Approximately NZD $50 per apostille.

How: Submit your original document by post or in person to MFAT in Wellington.

Processing time: Allow 1–3 weeks for apostille processing.

Important: Your police certificate must have been issued within 3 months of your Spanish Embassy appointment date. Plan your timing carefully — start the police check no more than 10 weeks before your appointment to ensure the certificate is still valid when you attend.

Total estimated timeline for NZ Police Vetting + MFAT apostille: 4–6 weeks. Factor this into your overall application schedule.

Consulate Appointment

The Spanish Embassy in Wellington

All New Zealand-based NLV applications are processed through the Spanish Embassy in Wellington. Unlike larger countries such as the USA or UK, New Zealand has only one Spanish diplomatic mission handling long-stay visas — so all NZ applicants regardless of location must attend in Wellington.

Embassy Details

Address: 56 Victoria Street, Wellington 6011, New Zealand

Appointments: Book via the Spanish Embassy's official website or the Spanish Ministry of Foreign Affairs appointment portal.

Appointment wait: Approximately 4–10 weeks. Fewer slots than larger consulates — book as early in your process as possible.

Tip: Check for appointment slots several weeks in advance and refresh regularly — cancellations appear without notice.

What to Bring to Your Appointment

  • Completed NLV application form (NIE/visa application)
  • Valid New Zealand passport + copy
  • 2 recent passport photographs
  • Apostilled NZ Police Vetting certificate
  • Medical certificate + apostille
  • Health insurance certificate (Spanish-approved, no co-pay)
  • Income evidence (bank statements, pension letters, etc.)
  • Proof of accommodation in Spain
  • Payment of the consular visa fee

Processing time after appointment: 1–3 months. Your application is forwarded to the Spanish Ministry of Foreign Affairs in Madrid for final assessment. You will not receive the visa at the appointment — it is posted or collected once approved.

Health Insurance

NLV Health Insurance for NZ Applicants

One of the core NLV requirements is private health insurance covering your entire stay in Spain, with no co-payments (sin copago) and no exclusions. This is a specific requirement and standard travel insurance does not qualify.

New Zealand domestic insurers — including Southern Cross Health Society and NIB — typically do not offer NLV-compliant Spanish resident health policies. Their products are designed for New Zealand healthcare, not for long-term residence in a foreign country.

What You Need

  • Policy valid in Spain for the full visa period
  • No co-payments (sin copago)
  • No significant exclusions (pre-existing conditions may be restricted)
  • Minimum coverage: typically €30,000 or unlimited
  • Emergency evacuation / repatriation cover

Recommended Approach

Most Kiwi NLV applicants use an international expat health insurer with Spain-specific resident coverage. Well-known providers operating in this space include Cigna Global, Aetna International, Allianz Care and similar. NZD & Currency

NZD Income and EUR Currency Conversion

Spanish consulates assess income in euros, based on the European Central Bank (ECB) reference rate at the time of application. The NZD/EUR exchange rate has fluctuated considerably in recent years, which creates an important planning consideration for New Zealand applicants.

2026 Approximate Rates

As of 2026, the approximate exchange rate is:

1 NZD ≈ 0.56 EUR

This means the €2,400/month NLV threshold equates to approximately NZD $4,300/month.

Always verify the current ECB rate before calculating — this figure is indicative only.

Build In a Buffer

If your NZD income converts to just above the €2,400 threshold, exchange rate movements could push you below it between your application and your consulate appointment.

Our recommendation: aim for income that converts to at least €2,600–2,800/month to provide a comfortable buffer. If your income falls short, savings can supplement — some consulates accept accessible savings in lieu of monthly income.

Common Questions

NLV from New Zealand — FAQ

Can New Zealand citizens apply for Spain's Non-Lucrative Visa?

Yes. New Zealand citizens are eligible for Spain's Non-Lucrative Visa. As non-EU nationals, New Zealanders must apply at the Spanish Embassy in Wellington before relocating to Spain. You must meet the income threshold (approximately €2,400/month for a single applicant), hold NLV-compliant health insurance, and provide an apostilled NZ Police Vetting certificate along with the standard document set.

Which consulate do New Zealand applicants use for the NLV?

All New Zealand applicants apply at the Spanish Embassy in Wellington at 56 Victoria Street, Wellington 6011. There are no other Spanish consulates in New Zealand that handle long-stay visa applications, so applicants from Auckland, Christchurch or any other city must attend in Wellington. Book your appointment early — slots are limited compared to larger countries.

What police certificate do I need as a New Zealander?

You need a New Zealand Police Vetting Service certificate (Criminal History Report), obtained through the NZ Police website at police.govt.nz. This must then be apostilled by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Trade (MFAT). The certificate must be issued within 3 months of your consulate appointment date. Allow 4–6 weeks total for the police check and apostille.

Does New Zealand Superannuation qualify as NLV income?

Yes, NZ Superannuation is a qualifying passive income source. However, the 2026 single-person NZ Super rate of approximately NZD $3,000/month (around €1,650/month) typically falls below the €2,400/month NLV threshold. Most NZ Super recipients need to supplement with KiwiSaver drawdowns, rental income or investment income to meet the full threshold.

How do I apostille my New Zealand documents for Spain?

New Zealand is a Hague Apostille Convention signatory, so NZ documents are apostilled by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Trade (MFAT). Submit your original document — police certificate, birth certificate, medical certificate — to MFAT with the apostille fee of approximately NZD $50 per document. Processing is 1–3 weeks by post or in person at MFAT in Wellington.

How long does the NLV process take from New Zealand?

Allow 4–6 months total: approximately 4–6 weeks for document preparation and police vetting/apostille, 4–10 weeks for a Wellington embassy appointment, and 1–3 months for processing in Madrid after your appointment. Start your application as early as possible to give yourself plenty of runway before your intended move date.

Can I travel to Europe while my NLV application is being processed?

New Zealanders can visit the Schengen Area visa-free for up to 90 days in any 180-day period. While your NLV is being processed, you may travel to Spain or Europe as a tourist within this 90-day limit, but you cannot establish residency or live there as if the visa were already granted. Wait until your visa sticker is issued before beginning your life in Spain.

Do I need to speak Spanish to apply for the NLV?

No Spanish language ability is required to apply for the Non-Lucrative Visa. The application process and all documentation can be managed in English. Our specialists at Platinum Legal Spain handle correspondence with the Spanish authorities on your behalf. Once you are in Spain, basic Spanish is very helpful for daily life, but it is not a legal requirement for the visa.
