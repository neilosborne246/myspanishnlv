The Process

How Our Managed NLV Application Works

From your eligibility check to your visa approval — here is exactly what happens when you use our managed service.

1

Eligibility Check — Free

Complete our online eligibility check via the dashboard. Share your income sources, amounts, nationality, and where you plan to apply. We assess whether you qualify and confirm the exact income threshold for your consulate and household size. No payment required at this stage.

2

Pay Deposit & Meet Your Case Manager (€400)

If you qualify, pay the €400 deposit to begin. You are assigned a dedicated immigration case manager from Platinum Legal Spain who will guide your application. They issue your tailored document checklist — specific to your nationality, consulate, and income type.

3

Prepare & Submit Your Documents (€400 before submission)

Gather the documents on your checklist. Your case manager reviews everything before submission. Certified translations up to €100 are included. When your application pack is complete and approved, the second payment of €400 is due and your application is submitted to the consulate.

4

Consulate Appointment & Processing

Most consulates require you to attend in person to submit your biometrics. Your case manager prepares you for what to expect and what to bring. Processing typically takes 1–3 months. You can track your application status in real time via the dashboard.

5

Visa Approved — Final Payment & Next Steps (€699)

When your visa is approved, the final payment of €699 is due. Your case manager provides guidance on what to do when you arrive in Spain — Padrón registration, NIE/TIE appointment, health registration, and other essentials. You are ready to start your new life in Spain.

Common Questions

NLV Services — Frequently Asked Questions

How much does your NLV application service cost in total?

Our managed NLV application service costs €1,499 total, paid in three stages: €400 deposit to begin, €400 before your application is submitted to the consulate, and €699 when your visa is approved. There are no hidden fees. You also pay government application fees directly (typically €80–€100) — these are separate from our service fee and are paid to the Spanish government, not to us.

What does the managed service include?

Our service includes: initial eligibility assessment, a dedicated immigration case manager, a tailored document checklist for your consulate, document review and preparation guidance, certified translations up to €100, pre-submission application review, consulate appointment guidance, real-time dashboard tracking, and post-approval guidance on arriving and settling in Spain. The service is end-to-end — you are not left to figure anything out alone.

Can you help if my NLV application was already refused?

Yes. Our appeal service begins with a free review of your refusal notice. Most NLV refusals are on income evidence, document, or health insurance grounds — all of which are addressable. We advise whether a formal appeal (recurso) or a strengthened fresh application is the better route for your situation, then prepare the strongest possible case for reconsideration.

Do you handle NLV renewals?

Yes — our renewal service costs €699 total (€300 + €399) and covers the full renewal process from within Spain. Renewals are submitted to the Spanish immigration authorities (Oficina de Extranjería), not the consulate. You need updated income evidence, a new health insurance certificate, and current Padrón documentation. The renewal is typically for 2 years, with a further 2-year renewal available after that.

Can you handle applications for my whole family?

Yes — our family application service handles one lead applicant plus dependants (spouse/partner and dependent children) in a single coordinated application. All family members apply together and receive their visas together. The income threshold increases by approximately €600/month per additional family member. Contact us for a family application assessment and quote specific to your family size.

Do I have to attend the consulate in person?

Most consulates require you to attend in person to submit your application and provide biometrics (fingerprints and photograph). Some consulates permit applications to be submitted by post or courier for the document stage with in-person attendance only for biometrics. Your case manager advises on the exact requirements for your specific consulate. We provide full guidance on what to bring and what to expect at your appointment.
