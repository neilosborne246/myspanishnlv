FBI Background Check Requirement for the Spanish Consulate Houston Texas NLV Application

For all US NLV applicants including those applying through Houston, the FBI check is the most time-sensitive document. Start it the moment you decide to apply.

Federal FBI check required — not a Texas DPS state check

Spanish consulates require the FBI Identity History Summary — the federal background check. A Texas Department of Public Safety (DPS) check or any other state-level check will not be accepted. Only the FBI check provides the nationwide criminal history summary the consulate requires. See full NLV requirements.

FBI check must be apostilled by the US Department of State

After receiving your FBI check, it must be apostilled by the US Department of State. A notarised or certified copy is not an apostille and will not be accepted by the Spanish consulate. The apostille confirms the document's authenticity for international use in Spain.

Allow 3–5 months for the complete FBI check and apostille process

Ordering the FBI check by mail takes approximately 2–4 months to receive. The Department of State apostille adds additional time. In total, allow 3–5 months for the complete FBI check and apostille process. FBI-approved channellers can expedite the FBI portion — our specialists will advise on the best option.

Spain Non Lucrative Visa Houston Consulate — Frequently Asked Questions

Which states does the Spanish Consulate in Houston serve for the NLV?

The Houston consulate has jurisdiction over: Arkansas, Kansas, Louisiana, Missouri, Nebraska, North Dakota, Oklahoma, South Dakota, and Texas. Legal residents of any of these states must apply for their NLV through the Houston consulate. This is determined by your state of legal residence — not your preference or proximity to another consulate.

Does the Houston consulate have better appointment availability than New York or Miami?

The Houston consulate is generally cited as having comparatively more appointment availability than the New York and Miami consulates, which handle very high demand. However, availability varies over time and can tighten quickly during peak periods. We recommend booking as early as possible regardless. If appointment availability is a key factor, our specialists can advise on current wait times across all US consulates when you apply.

What FBI check is required for the Spanish Consulate Houston Texas NLV application?

Spanish consulates require the FBI Identity History Summary — the federal background check. A Texas DPS check or any other state-level check will not be accepted. The FBI check must be apostilled by the US Department of State. Allow 3–5 months for the complete process. Our specialists flag this as the top priority from day one.

What Texas-specific income sources are accepted for the Spain NLV through Houston?

Texas applicants commonly use Social Security award letters, pension statements, 401k/IRA distributions, investment portfolio income, stock dividends, oil and gas royalty income, rental income, and other passive income sources. The threshold is approximately €2,400/month for a single applicant (plus ~€600/month per additional family member). USD amounts are converted to euros at the current rate. Our specialists advise on presenting your specific income mix effectively.

How long does the Spanish Consulate Houston take to process a Spain NLV?

Processing times at the Houston consulate typically range from 4–10 weeks after submission of a complete application. The legal maximum is 90 days. A thorough and correct application minimises the risk of delays. Our specialists ensure your dossier is complete and correctly presented before you attend your appointment. See the NLV processing time guide.

What documents should I bring to the Houston consulate NLV appointment?

Bring originals and copies of: valid passport, apostilled FBI Identity History Summary, income evidence (Social Security letters, pension statements, bank statements, investment/401k/IRA statements), NLV-compliant health insurance certificate, medical certificate, completed Modelo EX01 forms, and two passport photos. Consulate fees are payable on the day. Our specialists provide a precise checklist for the Houston consulate specifically.

Tips for the Spanish Consulate Houston Texas NLV application?

Key tips for the Houston consulate: take advantage of comparatively better appointment availability but still book early; order the FBI check immediately (3–5 month process); ensure the FBI check has a US Department of State apostille — not just a notarisation; bring both originals and copies of all documents; check passport validity before starting; and get a full dossier pre-check from our specialists before your appointment date.

What happens after my NLV appointment at the Houston consulate?

After your appointment, the Houston consulate processes your application — typically 4–10 weeks. You will be notified of the decision. If approved, your NLV is stamped in your passport. You then have 90 days to travel to Spain. Once in Spain, register your address (empadronamiento) and obtain your TIE residence card at the local police station within 30 days of arrival.
