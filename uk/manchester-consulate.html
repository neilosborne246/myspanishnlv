Consulate Information

Consulate of Spain in Manchester

Key information about the Manchester consulate — and what North of England applicants need to know before booking.

Consulate details

  • Official name: Consulate of Spain in Manchester
  • Address: 1A Westholme Street, Manchester M2 5EB (note: always verify the current address via the official consulate website before attending — addresses can change)
  • Catchment area: North of England (approximate — confirm your exact postcode coverage with the consulate directly)
  • Appointment booking: Via the official Spanish consulate appointment system (SEPIN) — visit the official consulate website for the current booking link
  • Contact: For current phone numbers, email addresses, and opening hours, refer to the official Consulate of Spain in Manchester website. Do not rely on third-party listings, which may be outdated.

Always verify current contact details, document requirements, opening hours, and fee information directly via the official Spanish consulate website before your appointment.

Manchester vs London — key differences

  • Generally better appointment availability — the Manchester consulate is less oversubscribed than London, particularly outside peak months
  • Smaller catchment area — serves the North of England, resulting in lower overall application volumes
  • Same core requirements — the fundamental NLV requirements are the same as for London; the differences are in processing volumes and specific document presentation preferences
  • Confirm your catchment area — if you are near the boundary between the Manchester and London catchment areas, confirm with both consulates which one covers your postcode before booking

What You Need

NLV document requirements — Manchester consulate

The core NLV requirements are the same across all Spanish consulates. Here is the full document checklist, with notes on Manchester-specific nuances.

Document checklist

  • Valid UK passport — original plus photocopies of all pages containing personal data, visas, or stamps. At least 1 year of validity beyond your intended entry into Spain.
  • Completed Modelo EX01 — the Spanish national long-stay visa application form, completed in full in Spanish. Plus any additional national visa form required by the Manchester consulate.
  • DBS certificate — apostilled by the FCDO Legalisation Office and translated into Spanish by a sworn translator. Must be no more than 3 months old at the date of your consulate appointment.
  • Medical certificate — from your GP, confirming absence of contagious disease, dated within 90 days of your appointment and translated into Spanish.
  • NLV-compliant health insurance — certificate from an authorised Spanish provider. No co-payments, no excess, minimum 1 year of full coverage. NHS, EHIC, GHIC, and travel insurance are all excluded.
  • Financial evidence — 6 months of bank statements plus all income evidence (pension letters, investment statements) demonstrating approximately €2,400/month passive income. All translated into Spanish.
  • Proof of accommodation in Spain — signed rental agreement or property deed for your Spanish address.
  • Two passport-format photographs — recent, white background, to consulate specification.

Manchester-specific notes

The Manchester consulate applies the same core legal requirements as all Spanish consulates, but as with every consulate, there are specific preferences and nuances that experience teaches. Our specialists are familiar with these and prepare your dossier accordingly.

Catchment area: The Manchester consulate's catchment area covers the North of England, but the exact boundary varies. If you are resident in areas such as the Midlands or Yorkshire, confirm whether you fall within the Manchester or London catchment area before booking your appointment. Applying at the wrong consulate will result in your application being refused at that consulate.

Appointment availability: Generally better than London. Slots can still fill up during peak periods, so we recommend monitoring availability actively once your document preparation is underway.

Document presentation: Bring originals and certified copies of every document. Keep documents well-organised, clearly tabbed, and in the order they are likely to be requested. Disorganised dossiers can slow the process at the desk.

Income evidence: Same requirements as all UK consulates — approximately €2,400/month passive income, evidenced with 6 months of statements. All documents require sworn Spanish translation (up to €100 included in our service fee).

Step by Step

The Manchester consulate appointment process

From document preparation to visa-in-hand — here is the complete process for Manchester NLV applicants.

1

Gather and prepare your documents

Work with your My Spanish NLV specialist to compile your complete dossier. This includes ordering your DBS check, obtaining and apostilling all certificates, arranging health insurance, gathering financial evidence, and completing all Spanish government forms. Your specialist prepares everything to the Manchester consulate's specific requirements and delivers your dossier ready to present.

Weeks 1–8
2

Book your appointment via SEPIN

Appointments are booked through the official SEPIN online booking system. Availability at Manchester is generally better than London, but slots can still be limited at peak times. Begin monitoring availability as soon as your documents are in preparation. Your specialist advises on timing to avoid your DBS certificate expiring before your appointment date.

As early as possible
3

Attend your appointment in Manchester

Attend in person at the consulate with your complete original documents and certified copies of everything. Arrive at least 15 minutes before your appointment. Dress professionally. All family members included in the application must attend together. Check the consulate's current entry rules — including any restrictions on mobile phones in the waiting area — before attending.

Appointment day
4

Pay the consulate fee and await decision

Pay the consulate visa fee on the day — your case manager will confirm the exact current figure before your appointment. Processing typically takes 4–10 weeks. Once approved, your passport is returned with the visa stamped inside. You then travel to Spain and collect your TIE residence card within 30 days of arrival.

4–10 weeks

Practical Advice

Tips for your Manchester consulate appointment

📍

Confirm your catchment area

Before booking your appointment, confirm that your postcode falls within the Manchester consulate's catchment area. The boundary between Manchester and London catchment areas is not always obvious, particularly for applicants in the Midlands and Yorkshire. Contact the consulate directly if you are unsure — applying at the wrong consulate wastes time and fees.

📅

Take advantage of better availability

The Manchester consulate typically has better appointment availability than London. Use this advantage by starting your document preparation promptly and booking your appointment as soon as a suitable slot appears. Even with better availability, peak periods (spring and summer) can see higher demand — plan ahead rather than leaving booking to the last minute.

📄

Bring originals and copies

Bring the original of every document in your dossier alongside certified copies. The consulate typically retains copies and returns originals, but be prepared for all eventualities. Organise documents clearly — tabbed, in the expected order, with nothing missing. A well-organised dossier makes a positive impression and speeds up the desk process.

⏰

Arrive early

Arrive at least 15 minutes before your appointment time. Late arrivals risk losing their slot entirely, which means rebooking — potentially weeks of additional delay. Account for travel time, parking, and any security checks. If you are travelling from further north, consider staying locally the night before your appointment to remove any travel risk.

👔

Dress professionally

A formal government appointment warrants professional dress. Smart business or smart casual attire is appropriate. Presenting yourself professionally signals that you are a serious applicant and creates a positive first impression with consulate staff.

💷

Confirm payment method in advance

Consulate fees are paid on the day of your appointment. Check the official consulate website for the current accepted payment methods before attending — this can change and is not always cash. Your case manager will confirm the current fee amount before your appointment.

Common Questions

Manchester consulate FAQ

Who applies at the Spanish Consulate in Manchester for the NLV?

Applicants who are resident in the North of England apply for Spain's Non-Lucrative Visa at the Consulate of Spain in Manchester. The exact catchment area should be confirmed directly with the consulate, as the boundary with the London catchment area can be unclear for some postcodes — particularly in the Midlands and Yorkshire. Scotland residents apply at the Edinburgh consulate; the rest of England and Wales applies in London.

Is the Manchester consulate less busy than London for NLV appointments?

Yes, generally. The Manchester consulate serves a smaller catchment area and tends to have better appointment availability than London, particularly during peak spring and summer months. Applicants in the North of England who might be tempted to travel to London should apply at Manchester — applying at the wrong consulate will result in your application being refused, regardless of the quality of your documents. The appointment advantage at Manchester is a genuine benefit worth using.

Are the document requirements different at Manchester compared to London?

The core legal requirements for the NLV are the same at all Spanish consulates — the same income threshold, the same health insurance standard, the same criminal record requirements, and the same government forms. Where consulates differ is in how strictly they apply these requirements, specific document presentation preferences, and any additional documents they may request. Our specialists are familiar with Manchester consulate preferences and prepare your dossier accordingly.

How long does the Manchester consulate take to process an NLV?

Processing times at the Manchester consulate are generally in the range of 4–10 weeks from appointment date, though this varies with demand. As with all consulates, we advise against making irreversible commitments — ending your tenancy, booking one-way flights, or making major financial arrangements — until your visa is confirmed and physically in your passport.

How much is the consulate fee for the NLV at Manchester?

Consulate fees are set by the Spanish Ministry of Foreign Affairs and vary. Your case manager will confirm the exact current figure before your appointment. The fee is paid on the day at the consulate — confirm the accepted payment methods via the official consulate website before attending, as requirements can change.

Do I need to bring originals or just copies to the Manchester consulate?

Bring both originals and certified copies of every document. The consulate typically retains copies and returns originals, but this can vary. Never attend with copies alone — if originals are requested and you cannot produce them, your appointment may not proceed. Keep documents clearly tabbed and organised in the expected order.

How far in advance should I book my Manchester consulate NLV appointment?

Book as early as possible once your documents are in preparation — ideally 6–8 weeks before you want to attend. Manchester generally has better availability than London, but slots during peak periods (March–August) can still be limited. Be mindful that your DBS certificate and medical certificate are time-limited (both typically no more than 3 months old at appointment date), so coordinate your appointment booking with your document preparation timeline.

Can someone attend the Manchester consulate appointment on my behalf?

No. NLV appointments at the Spanish Consulate in Manchester require the applicant to attend in person — the consulate needs to verify your identity and take your biometric data. There is no provision for a representative to attend in your place. All family members included in a joint application must attend together on the same appointment.
