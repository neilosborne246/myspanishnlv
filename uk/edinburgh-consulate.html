Consulate Information

Consulate-General of Spain in Edinburgh

Key information about the Edinburgh consulate — and what Scottish applicants need to know before booking their appointment.

Consulate details

  • Official name: Consulate-General of Spain in Edinburgh
  • Address: 63 North Castle Street, Edinburgh EH2 3LJ (note: always verify the current address via the official consulate website before attending — addresses can change)
  • Catchment area: Scotland (and potentially Northern Ireland — confirm with the consulate directly as catchment arrangements can vary)
  • Appointment booking: Via the official Spanish consulate appointment system (SEPIN) — visit the official consulate website for the current booking link
  • Contact: For current phone numbers, email addresses, and opening hours, refer to the official Consulate-General of Spain in Edinburgh website. Do not rely on third-party listings, which may be outdated.

Always verify current contact details, document requirements, opening hours, and fee information directly via the official consulate website before your appointment. Procedures can change without notice.

Why the Edinburgh consulate is worth knowing about

  • Generally efficient and well-organised — the Edinburgh consulate has a good reputation among applicants and is considered one of the smoother UK consulate experiences
  • Better appointment availability than London — lower overall volume of applications means you are less likely to face a long wait for a slot
  • Same core requirements — Scotland is part of the UK; the NLV requirements are identical to those applying at London and Manchester
  • Catchment area questions — if you are near the Scottish-English border, or in Northern Ireland, confirm your catchment consulate before booking

What You Need

NLV requirements — Edinburgh consulate

The requirements are the same as at all Spanish consulates. Here is the full checklist, with notes on any Scotland-specific considerations.

Document checklist

  • Valid UK passport — original plus photocopies of all pages containing personal data, visas, or stamps. At least 1 year of validity beyond your intended entry into Spain.
  • Completed Modelo EX01 — the Spanish national long-stay visa application form, completed in full in Spanish. Plus any additional national visa form required by the Edinburgh consulate.
  • Criminal record certificate — apostilled and translated into Spanish. See the Scotland-specific notes on the right regarding which issuing body to use.
  • Medical certificate — from your GP, confirming absence of contagious disease. Dated within 90 days of your appointment and translated into Spanish.
  • NLV-compliant health insurance — from an authorised Spanish provider. No co-payments, no excess, minimum 1 year of full coverage. NHS, EHIC, GHIC, and travel insurance are all excluded.
  • Financial evidence — 6 months of bank statements plus all income evidence (pension letters, investment statements) demonstrating approximately €2,400/month passive income. All translated into Spanish.
  • Proof of accommodation in Spain — signed rental agreement or property deed for your Spanish address.
  • Two passport-format photographs — recent, white background, to consulate specification.

Scotland-specific notes

Criminal record certificate — important for Scottish applicants: In Scotland, criminal record checks may be processed through Disclosure Scotland rather than the DBS (Disclosure and Barring Service), which is the England and Wales body. Before ordering your check, confirm with the Edinburgh consulate which issuing body and which level of disclosure they require. Applying for the wrong type of check — or from the wrong body — can cause delays and necessitate reapplying. Your My Spanish NLV specialist will advise you on this during document preparation.

In all other respects, the criminal record certificate must be apostilled by the FCDO Legalisation Office and translated into Spanish by a sworn translator — the same as for all UK applicants.

Income evidence: Scottish income sources — Scottish widows pensions, Clydesdale bank statements, Scottish investment accounts — are all perfectly acceptable and treated identically to income from English banks and pension providers. The Spanish consulate assesses income in euros at the ECB rate, regardless of currency or institution.

NHS Scotland: NHS Scotland entitlement does not satisfy the NLV health insurance requirement — the same as for NHS England, Wales, or Northern Ireland. You require private health insurance from an authorised Spanish provider with no co-payments and full coverage.

Step by Step

The Edinburgh consulate appointment process

From document preparation to visa-in-hand — here is the complete process for Scottish NLV applicants.

1

Gather and prepare your documents

Work with your My Spanish NLV specialist to compile your complete dossier. This includes confirming which criminal record check to order (DBS or Disclosure Scotland), obtaining and apostilling all certificates, arranging health insurance, gathering financial evidence, and completing all Spanish government forms. Your specialist prepares everything to the Edinburgh consulate's requirements and delivers your dossier ready to present.

Weeks 1–8
2

Book your appointment via SEPIN

Appointments are booked through the official SEPIN online booking system. Availability at Edinburgh is generally better than London. Begin monitoring the system as soon as your documents are in preparation. Your specialist advises on timing to ensure your criminal record certificate does not expire before your appointment date.

As early as possible
3

Attend your appointment in Edinburgh

Attend in person at 63 North Castle Street (verify current address on the official website) with your complete original documents and certified copies of everything. Arrive at least 15 minutes before your appointment. Dress professionally. All family members included in the application must attend together. Check the consulate's current entry rules before attending.

Appointment day
4

Pay the consulate fee and await decision

Pay the consulate visa fee on the day — your case manager will confirm the exact current figure before your appointment. Processing typically takes 4–10 weeks. Once approved, your passport is returned with the visa stamped inside. You then travel to Spain and collect your TIE residence card within 30 days of arrival.

4–10 weeks

Practical Advice

Tips for your Edinburgh consulate appointment

📋

Confirm your criminal record check type

Before ordering your criminal record certificate, confirm with the Edinburgh consulate whether they accept a Disclosure Scotland certificate, a DBS certificate, or either. This is particularly important because applying for the wrong type can cause delays. Our specialists flag this during your initial document preparation and ensure the correct certificate is ordered for your consulate.

📍

Confirm your catchment consulate

Scottish residents apply at Edinburgh — but if you live near the English border, in Northern Ireland, or in any other area where the catchment might be unclear, confirm directly with the consulate before booking. The Edinburgh consulate may also serve Northern Ireland applicants — verify current arrangements directly. Applying at the wrong consulate results in a refusal.

📄

Bring originals and certified copies

Bring the original of every document in your dossier alongside certified copies. The consulate typically retains copies and returns originals. Keep documents well-organised, clearly tabbed, and in the expected order. A well-prepared, clearly structured dossier signals professionalism and speeds up the desk process.

⏰

Arrive 15 minutes early

Arrive at least 15 minutes before your appointment to allow for security checks and registration. If you are travelling from further afield — Inverness, Aberdeen, or the Highlands — consider staying in Edinburgh the night before to remove any risk of travel delays affecting your appointment.

👔

Dress professionally

This is a formal government appointment. Smart business or smart casual attire is appropriate. A professional presentation signals that you are a serious applicant and creates a positive first impression with consulate staff — this is good practice regardless of which consulate you attend.

💷

Confirm payment method in advance

Consulate fees are paid on the day of your appointment. Check the official consulate website for the current accepted payment methods before attending — requirements can change and are not always cash. Your case manager will confirm the current fee amount so there are no surprises on the day.

Common Questions

Edinburgh consulate FAQ

Who applies at the Spanish Consulate in Edinburgh for the NLV?

Applicants who are resident in Scotland apply for Spain's Non-Lucrative Visa at the Consulate-General of Spain in Edinburgh. England and Wales residents (except those in the North of England) apply in London. North of England residents apply in Manchester. The Edinburgh consulate may also cover Northern Ireland — confirm current arrangements directly with the consulate. You must apply at the consulate covering your area of residence, not the geographically nearest consulate.

Does living in Scotland affect my NLV application?

No. Scotland is part of the United Kingdom, and British nationals in Scotland are subject to exactly the same NLV requirements as applicants in England or Wales. The income thresholds, health insurance requirements, document standards, and overall process are all identical. The only practical difference is that Scottish residents apply through the Edinburgh consulate, which is generally considered an efficient and well-run consulate with good appointment availability.

Do I go to Edinburgh or London if I live near the Scottish-English border?

It depends on which side of the border you are currently resident. If you live anywhere in Scotland — including the Scottish Borders, Dumfries and Galloway, and other border regions — you apply at Edinburgh. If you live in the North of England — including Northumberland, Cumbria, and other northern English counties — you apply at Manchester. If you are genuinely uncertain about your catchment area, contact both consulates directly before booking any appointment. Applying at the wrong consulate will result in your application being refused at that consulate, requiring you to start again.

What criminal record certificate do Scottish applicants need?

Scottish applicants need a criminal record certificate apostilled by the FCDO Legalisation Office and translated into Spanish by a sworn translator — the same as all UK applicants. The question specific to Scotland is which body issues the certificate: the DBS (Disclosure and Barring Service, which covers England and Wales) or Disclosure Scotland. Before ordering your check, confirm with the Edinburgh consulate which they require. Our specialists will advise you on this during the document preparation stage and ensure the correct certificate is obtained and processed correctly.

Is the Edinburgh consulate efficient for NLV applications?

Yes. The Consulate-General of Spain in Edinburgh has a generally positive reputation among NLV applicants. It is considered one of the more efficient UK Spanish consulates, with better appointment availability than London and typically reasonable processing times. That said, every application is different and processing times can vary — we still recommend preparing your documents thoroughly, allowing generous timelines, and not making irreversible commitments until your visa is physically in your passport.

How far in advance should I book my Edinburgh consulate NLV appointment?

Book your appointment as early as possible once your documents are in preparation — ideally 6–8 weeks before you want to attend. Edinburgh generally has better availability than London, but slots can still fill during peak periods (spring and summer). Your criminal record certificate and medical certificate are typically valid for only 3 months from issue, so coordinate your appointment booking carefully with your document preparation timeline to avoid needing to reorder certificates.

How long does the Edinburgh consulate take to process an NLV application?

Processing times at the Edinburgh consulate are generally in the range of 4–10 weeks from appointment date. The consulate's lower application volume compared to London can mean faster processing in practice, but this varies with seasonal demand. Do not make irreversible commitments — ending a tenancy, booking one-way flights, or similar — until your visa is confirmed and physically stamped in your passport.

What should I bring to my Edinburgh consulate NLV appointment?

Bring originals and certified copies of every document: passport, criminal record certificate (apostilled and translated), medical certificate (translated, within 90 days), NLV-compliant health insurance certificate, 6 months of bank statements and income evidence (all translated), proof of Spanish accommodation, completed Modelo EX01, national visa form, and two passport photographs. Organise everything clearly — tabbed and in order. Bring payment for the consulate fee (confirm accepted methods in advance via the official consulate website).
