NLV Health Insurance Requirements

These are not suggestions — they are mandatory conditions. Any policy that does not meet every one of these criteria will cause your NLV application to be rejected.

  • ✅
    Comprehensive coverage in Spain — must cover general practitioner, specialist consultations, hospitalisations, emergency treatment, diagnostic tests, and surgery.
  • ✅
    No co-payments (copagos) — you must not be required to pay any portion of the cost when you access healthcare. Even a small co-payment makes the policy non-compliant.
  • ✅
    No excess or deductible — you must not have to pay a set amount before the insurance covers your costs. Any excess, however small, is a disqualifying feature.
  • ✅
    Provider authorised to operate in Spain — the insurance company must be registered with Spain's Directorate-General of Insurance (DGSFP) and authorised to operate in Spain. Many international insurers are not.
  • ✅
    Covers the full duration of the visa — the policy must be valid for at least the full duration of the visa being applied for (typically one year for the initial NLV). A policy that expires before the visa does is insufficient.
  • ❌
    Travel insurance does not qualify — however comprehensive it may be. Travel insurance is not the same as private health insurance and will be rejected by the consulate without exception.

What to Look For in an NLV Health Insurance Policy

Beyond the mandatory requirements, these are the features that matter most for your daily life in Spain.

💊

No Co-payments (Copagos)

The absolute must-have. Every consultation, prescription, and treatment must be covered in full by the insurer. A policy that requires you to contribute even €5 per visit fails the NLV requirement.

🚫

No Excess or Deductible

Equally non-negotiable. There must be no threshold amount that you pay before insurance kicks in. Zero excess, zero deductible — on every claim.

🗺️

Spain-Wide Coverage

Your policy must provide comprehensive coverage across all of Spain — not just in specific regions or cities. Check that coverage extends to wherever you plan to live, including the Canary Islands, Balearics, Ceuta, and Melilla if relevant.

🏛️

Provider Authorised in Spain

The insurer must be registered with and authorised by Spain's DGSFP (Dirección General de Seguros y Fondos de Pensiones). This is a regulatory requirement — not all well-known international insurers qualify.

🗣️

English-Language Service

Many expats benefit significantly from being able to contact their insurer in English when navigating the Spanish healthcare system. Check that customer service and claims handling are available in English.

👨‍👩‍👧

Family Plans Available

If you are applying as a family, a family plan covering all members on a single policy is often more cost-effective and easier to manage than separate individual policies. Check family plan options and per-person pricing before committing.

How Much Does NLV Health Insurance Cost?

💰

Typical Cost Range

NLV-compliant health insurance typically costs €500–€2,000 per person per year. The exact premium depends on several factors:

  • Age: younger applicants pay significantly less; premiums rise with age
  • Health status: pre-existing conditions may affect eligibility or pricing
  • Level of cover: basic compliant cover is cheaper; comprehensive cover with dental, vision, and mental health is more
  • Location in Spain: some regions have different pricing structures
  • Insurer: premiums vary between providers for equivalent cover levels

When to Buy Your NLV Health Insurance

📅

The Right Timing Window

Buy your health insurance after your eligibility has been confirmed and your document dossier is approaching completion — but not so early that the policy expires before your visa is granted.

Most NLV-compliant policies are annual. If you buy your policy six months before your consulate appointment and your visa takes a further three months to process, your policy start date will be approximately nine months before you arrive in Spain — meaning it may need renewing before your first year in Spain is complete. This can create unnecessary complications.

Our case managers advise on the optimal timing for your health insurance purchase based on your specific application timeline and consulate. This is part of the pre-dossier preparation service.

⚠️

Do Not Buy Too Early or Too Late

Too early: your policy may expire before your visa is approved, forcing you to renew before you even arrive in Spain — and potentially creating a gap in the coverage dates shown on your certificate.

Too late: you cannot attend your consulate appointment without a valid health insurance certificate. Buying at the last minute risks choosing in haste and getting a non-compliant policy. Allow at least two weeks to research, compare, and purchase.

Our recommended timing: purchase your health insurance approximately four to six weeks before your consulate appointment, after your dossier is in final preparation. Your case manager will confirm the best timing for your application.

Common Health Insurance Mistakes That Cause NLV Rejection

Wrong health insurance is the single most common cause of NLV rejection. Here are the mistakes we see repeatedly — and how to avoid them:

  • Buying travel insurance instead of health insurance. Travel insurance — even comprehensive annual travel insurance — does not meet the NLV health insurance requirement. The consulate will reject it. You need private health insurance, not travel insurance.
  • Buying a policy with an excess. Many standard international health insurance policies include an excess (e.g., €500 per claim or per year). Any excess, however small, makes the policy non-compliant for the NLV. Read the policy terms carefully — or use a specialist who already knows which policies are compliant.
  • Buying from a provider not authorised in Spain. Some well-known international insurers are not authorised to operate in Spain. An otherwise comprehensive policy from a non-authorised provider will be rejected. Verify authorisation with your insurer or use a specialist comparison service that only offers compliant policies.
  • Buying too early and letting the coverage expire. A policy that starts too early relative to your consulate appointment may expire before your visa validity period ends — creating a gap. Time your purchase carefully with guidance from your case manager.
  • Not covering all family members. Every family member on your NLV application requires their own health insurance certificate. Forgetting to insure children or a spouse is a surprisingly common oversight.
  • Assuming your existing insurer is compliant. If you already have international health insurance, do not assume it meets NLV requirements. Check specifically: no co-payments, no excess, Spain authorisation. Many policies that are excellent for other purposes fail one or more of these criteria.

Frequently Asked Questions

    What health insurance is accepted for the Spain NLV?

    NLV-compliant health insurance must provide comprehensive coverage in Spain, with no co-payments, no excess, and from a provider authorised to operate in Spain. The policy must cover the full duration of the visa applied for. Travel insurance, policies with co-payments, policies with an excess, and policies from non-authorised providers are all rejected. Use a specialist comparison service such as spanish-healthinsurance.com or 247expatinsurance.com to ensure you get a compliant policy.

    Can I use the NHS (or my home country's health service) for the NLV?

    No. The NLV requires private health insurance from a provider authorised to operate in Spain. Access to the NHS, Medicare, or any other home-country state health service does not satisfy this requirement. The requirement is specifically for private health insurance in Spain — because as an NLV holder you will not have access to Spain's public healthcare system (until you qualify for it through long-term residency or other routes).

    How much does NLV health insurance cost per year?

    NLV-compliant health insurance typically costs between €500 and €2,000 per person per year. The exact figure depends on your age, health status, the level of cover you choose (basic compliant vs comprehensive with dental and vision), your location in Spain, and the insurer. Younger applicants typically pay towards the lower end; older applicants and those with pre-existing conditions may pay more. Get a personalised comparison quote from spanish-healthinsurance.com or 247expatinsurance.com for an accurate figure.

    When should I buy my NLV health insurance?

    Buy your health insurance after your eligibility is confirmed and your dossier is approaching completion — typically four to six weeks before your consulate appointment. Buying too early risks the policy expiring before your visa period is complete. Buying too late risks rushing the decision and purchasing a non-compliant policy. Your case manager will advise on the optimal timing for your specific application and consulate.

    What if I get sick before I move to Spain?

    If you become ill or receive a new diagnosis before your health insurance policy starts, this may be treated as a pre-existing condition by the insurer, which could affect coverage or premiums at the time of renewal. If you become seriously ill before your consulate appointment, you may need medical advice on whether to proceed with your visa application. Our case managers can advise on the implications for your NLV application. It is important to disclose any pre-existing conditions honestly when purchasing your policy.

    Does my whole family need health insurance?

    Yes. Every family member included in your NLV application must have their own NLV-compliant health insurance. The consulate requires a certificate for each applicant — both the main applicant and all dependants including children. Many insurers offer family plans that cover all family members on a single policy, which is often more cost-effective than purchasing separate individual policies. Enquire specifically about family plans when getting quotes from spanish-healthinsurance.com and 247expatinsurance.com.

    Which is better for NLV health insurance — spanish-healthinsurance.com or 247expatinsurance.com?

    Both are specialist services we recommend that understand the NLV requirements exactly. Spanish Health Insurance focuses specifically on Spain and offers comparison across multiple compliant policies. 247 Expat Insurance covers a broader range of expat insurance and is particularly strong for applicants with pre-existing conditions. We recommend getting a quote from both and comparing. Our case managers verify whichever policy you choose is consulate-compliant before your dossier is submitted.

    Can I use my EHIC or GHIC card instead of private health insurance for the NLV?

    No. The European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) or Global Health Insurance Card (GHIC) does not satisfy the NLV health insurance requirement. These cards provide access to state healthcare during temporary stays — they are not private health insurance and are not accepted by Spanish consulates for the NLV. You need a full private health insurance policy from a provider authorised in Spain, with no co-payments and no excess.
