How Long Does Spain Non Lucrative Visa Take?

How long does Spain non lucrative visa take? The honest answer: the consulate has up to 90 days to decide, but the full journey from first step to living in Spain typically takes four to six months. Here is a complete breakdown of every stage and what affects your timeline.

Spain NLV Timeline 2026 — Three Phases You Need to Understand

Each phase has a different driver — and a different level of control you can exercise over the timing.

📋

Phase 1: Preparation with Our Immigration Specialists

Typical duration: 4–8 weeks

This covers eligibility check, document gathering, apostilles, sworn translations, health insurance, dossier review, and consulate briefing. The speed here is largely within your control — the faster you gather documents and respond to our requests, the faster this phase moves. See our full step-by-step guide for detail on each stage.

🏛️

Phase 2: Spain NLV Consulate Processing Time

Typical duration: 4–8 weeks (up to 90 days)

Once your application is submitted at the consulate, processing time is entirely in the consulate's hands. The legal maximum is 90 calendar days, but many consulates decide within four to eight weeks. This is the one phase you cannot influence — which makes submitting a complete dossier even more important, as queries add to this time.

Note: consulate timelines vary significantly by location and season. Our specialists advise on timing strategy for your consulate.

✅

Phase 3: Notification, Visa Collection, and Travel

Typical duration: 1–2 weeks

Once a decision is made, the consulate notifies you — by email, phone, or online portal depending on the consulate. If approved, you collect your passport (or it is returned by post). This phase is usually quick, though postal returns can add a few days. After collection, you can travel to Spain within the validity window printed on your visa.

Spain NLV Processing Time 2026 — Consulate-by-Consulate Differences

Not all Spanish consulates are equal when it comes to processing speed and appointment availability.

Consulates with Longer Queues and Slower Processing

London and New York are the two busiest Spanish consulates for NLV applications. Both handle very high volumes, and appointment slots can be scarce — particularly during peak periods from January to March (when many people plan their Spanish move) and again in the summer months. If you are applying through London or New York, building extra time into your planning is essential.

Los Angeles and Miami also process significant volumes, and appointment availability can vary. See our dedicated guides for the Miami consulate and Los Angeles consulate. Our specialists keep track of current wait times and advise on the best strategy for your consulate.

Consulates with Faster Processing and Better Appointment Availability

Edinburgh and Houston have historically processed NLV applications more quickly than the busiest consulates. Smaller consulates serving lower-volume catchment areas — such as those in Australia and some European countries — can also move faster. The Houston consulate in particular is often noted for comparatively better appointment availability than New York or Miami.

Appointment availability itself can add weeks to your total timeline before the formal 90-day processing clock even starts. Booking your appointment as early as possible — ideally as soon as your dossier enters the final review stage — is one of the most effective ways to reduce your total waiting time.

Our immigration specialists advise on timing strategy. Based on your consulate, the time of year, and current appointment availability, we recommend the optimal point to book your appointment — so you are not sitting on a complete dossier waiting weeks for a slot, nor rushing your documents to meet a premature appointment date.

What Slows Down a Non Lucrative Visa Spain Application?

Most delays are avoidable. Here are the most common causes and how we help you avoid them.

📄

Missing or Incorrectly Formatted Documents

Documents submitted in the wrong format, without an apostille, with an expired date, or without a sworn translation are the single most common cause of delay. Our pre-submission dossier review catches these issues before you reach the consulate. See NLV requirements for what is needed.

🔖

Apostille Processing Delays from Issuing Authorities

In some countries, obtaining apostilles from government authorities can take two to four weeks or longer. UK criminal records, for example, require apostilling from the FCDO — which has its own processing queue. Order these early and factor in the time.

🏥

Wrong Health Insurance Policy Type

Buying the wrong type of health insurance — travel insurance, a policy with an excess, or a policy from a provider not authorised to operate in Spain — is one of the top reasons NLV applications are rejected outright. NLV-compliant policies typically cost €500–€2,000/year. We check your policy before your dossier is finalised and can refer you to spanish-healthinsurance.com or 247expatinsurance.com.

📅

Peak Consulate Periods (January–March and Summer)

January to March is the peak period for NLV applications, as many people plan their relocation to Spain for the following summer. Consulate appointment availability tightens, and processing queues lengthen. Applying outside this window — or booking your appointment well in advance — helps.

❓

Additional Queries Raised by the Consulate

If the consulate raises a query about your application — requesting additional evidence of income, clarification on a document, or further information — this pauses the processing clock while you respond. Fast, correct responses to queries are essential. Our team handles these for you.

👨‍👩‍👧

Family Applications Requiring More Documents

Applications that include family members require additional documents for each applicant. Coordinating multiple sets of documents, apostilles, and translations takes longer than a single-person application. The income threshold also rises by approximately €600/month per additional family member. Allow additional time for family applications.

How Long Does Spain Non Lucrative Visa Take — Frequently Asked Questions

How long does Spain non lucrative visa take in total in 2026?

Allow four to six months from starting the process to arriving in Spain with your visa stamped in your passport. This includes four to eight weeks of preparation with our team, up to 90 days of consulate processing (typically four to eight weeks), and one to two weeks for notification and collection. Timelines vary by consulate and time of year.

How long does the consulate take to decide on my Spain NLV application?

The Spanish consulate has up to 90 calendar days by law to reach a decision on a non lucrative visa application. In practice, many consulates process applications within four to eight weeks. The timeline varies considerably by location, season, and application volume. There is no way to pay to expedite this period — it is fixed by Spanish administrative law.

How long does preparation with a specialist take?

Preparation with My Spanish NLV typically takes four to eight weeks. This covers your eligibility check, document gathering (including ordering criminal record certificates), apostilles, sworn Spanish translations, health insurance verification, full dossier pre-check, and consulate briefing. The preparation phase is largely within your control — the faster you gather documents and respond to our requests, the faster this phase moves.

Which consulate processes NLV applications fastest?

Processing times vary and change over time, so we recommend asking our team for current intelligence on your specific consulate. Historically, Edinburgh and Houston have been faster than London and New York. Smaller consulates serving lower-volume areas also tend to process more quickly. Our specialists track current wait times and advise accordingly.

What slows down a Spain NLV application?

The most common delay causes are: missing or incorrectly formatted documents, apostille processing delays, wrong health insurance policy type, additional queries from the consulate, peak periods (January–March and summer), and incomplete income documentation. Our pre-submission dossier review is designed specifically to catch and resolve these issues before you submit.

Can I check the status of my Spain NLV application?

Yes. Most Spanish consulates provide an online tracking portal where you can check the status of your application using the reference number provided at submission. Our case managers also actively monitor your application throughout the waiting period and will alert you immediately when a decision is made or if any action is required from you.

What if the consulate does not respond within 90 days?

Under Spanish administrative procedure, if no decision is issued within the legal timeframe, the application may be treated as refused by administrative silence (silencio administrativo negativo). This outcome is rare but can be challenged. If your application approaches or exceeds 90 days without a decision, contact our team immediately — we will advise on the appropriate steps, which may include formal enquiries to the consulate or an administrative appeal.

Is there a faster track or priority processing option for the Spain NLV?

No. There is no premium or priority processing option for Spain's Non-Lucrative Visa. The consulate processing period (up to 90 days) is set by Spanish administrative law and cannot be shortened by any payment or request. The only ways to reduce your total timeline are to prepare a complete, error-free dossier (avoiding resubmissions and queries) and to book your consulate appointment as promptly as possible once your documents are ready.
