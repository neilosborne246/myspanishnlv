Quick Reference

Key Renewal Dates to Remember

3-4 months before expiration: Contact your consulate to schedule your renewal appointment. Don't wait until the last minute—appointment availability can be limited.

2-3 months before expiration: Complete your renewal appointment and submit your application. This gives you a buffer if processing takes longer than expected.

1 month before expiration: Contact the consulate to confirm your application is processing on schedule.

At expiration: If your permit expires but renewal is still processing, don't panic. You have a de facto grace period while your application is pending. Keep all documentation with you.

Essential Documentation Checklist

Have these ready for your renewal appointment:

  • Valid passport
  • Current NLV (the permit you're renewing)
  • 3 months of current bank statements
  • Proof of current income
  • Current health insurance documentation
  • Housing documentation (lease or property deed)
  • Padrón certificate (from your town hall)
  • Any correspondence from the consulate
