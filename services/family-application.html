Who Can Join Your Non Lucrative Visa Spain Family Application?

Spanish immigration law allows the lead applicant to include certain family members in a joint NLV application.

💍

Spouse or Civil Partner — Always Eligible

A legally married spouse or registered civil partner can always be included in the lead applicant's NLV application. Both must meet the health insurance requirement and provide their own passport and documentation.

👧

Dependent Children Under 18

Children under 18 at the time of application can be included. Their documentation requirements are simpler than adult family members — birth certificate (apostilled and translated), passport, and health insurance are the primary requirements.

🎓

Financially Dependent Adult Children

Children over 18 may be included on the application if they are financially dependent on the lead applicant due to a disability or other recognised dependency. This is assessed case by case — contact us to discuss your situation.

👴

Dependent Parents (Case by Case)

In some circumstances, dependent parents of the lead applicant may be included. This is assessed on a case-by-case basis and depends on demonstrating genuine financial dependency. Contact us to discuss whether this applies to your family.

How Much Income Does a Family Need for Spain's Non-Lucrative Visa?

The financial requirement increases for each family member included in your non lucrative visa Spain family application.

Lead Applicant

Approximately €2,400/month in provable passive income or equivalent savings. This is the baseline requirement for a single-person NLV application.

Each Additional Family Member

Approximately +€600/month per additional family member. A couple would therefore need approximately €3,000/month; a family of four approximately €4,200/month.

Updated Annually

These figures are linked to the Spanish IPREM indicator and updated every year. Exact figures for your application year are confirmed at your eligibility assessment — do not rely solely on figures from previous years.

Not sure if your family meets the threshold? Our eligibility assessment is the first milestone of the service — we confirm your financial position against the current year's requirements before you commit to gathering all your family's documents. Non Lucrative Visa Spain Family Application — Frequently Asked Questions

Who can join a non lucrative visa Spain family application?

A spouse or registered civil partner can always join the lead applicant's non lucrative visa Spain family application. Dependent children under 18 can also be included. Children over 18 may be included if they are financially dependent due to a disability or other recognised dependency. Dependent parents may be included on a case-by-case basis — contact us to discuss your specific family circumstances.

How much income does a family need for Spain's Non-Lucrative Visa?

The income requirement for the Spain NLV family application increases for each additional family member. The threshold is approximately €2,400/month for the lead applicant, plus approximately €600/month per additional family member included. These figures are linked to the Spanish IPREM indicator and are updated annually. A couple would need approximately €3,000/month; a family of four approximately €4,200/month.

What documents do children need for a Spain NLV family application?

Children typically need their birth certificate (apostilled and translated into Spanish), a valid passport, proof of private health insurance compliant with Spanish NLV requirements, and a recent passport photo. For older children, some consulates also request school enrolment letters or academic records. The exact requirements vary by consulate — your case manager will provide a tailored document checklist for each family member.

Can we apply at different consulates if we live in different countries?

Each applicant must apply at the Spanish consulate with jurisdiction over their area of residence. If family members are living in different countries or different consulate jurisdictions, they may need to submit their applications to different consulates. We manage this complexity — our immigration specialists are familiar with requirements across multiple consulates and coordinate accordingly to ensure all family members' dossiers meet their specific consulate's requirements.

What happens if one family member's application is refused?

If one family member's application is refused while others are approved, that person cannot travel to Spain on the NLV. We work to minimise this risk by ensuring all family members' documentation is correct and complete before submission. If a refusal does occur, we review the specific grounds and advise on whether to appeal formally or reapply with a strengthened dossier. See our NLV appeal service for more information.

Can children study in Spain on the family NLV?

Children included in a family NLV application may attend school in Spain — in fact, school-age children are typically required to enrol in education after arriving. However, the NLV does not permit working for a Spanish employer. Children who reach working age while in Spain on the NLV cannot take up employment without changing their visa status.

How much does the non lucrative visa Spain family application service cost?

The lead applicant pays our standard service fee of €1,499 (paid across three milestones: €400 eligibility and onboarding, €400 document preparation, €699 submission and support). Additional adult family members are priced on enquiry depending on your specific family circumstances. Children's inclusion costs are lower as their documentation needs are simpler. Contact us for a tailored family quote.

We are not married — can my partner still join my Spain NLV family application?

Unmarried partners who are not in a registered civil partnership face a harder route on the NLV. Spanish immigration law recognises married spouses and registered civil partners as eligible family members for joint applications. If you are in a long-term relationship but not legally married or registered, you may need to formalise your partnership before applying, or explore whether your partner can apply separately as an independent applicant. Contact us to discuss your specific situation and the options available.
