Who Should Apply for a Non Lucrative Visa Spain?

The Non-Lucrative Visa is Spain's most popular route to long-term residency for non-EU citizens who do not need to work. To apply for the non lucrative visa Spain, you must demonstrate passive income of approximately €2,400/month.

🌴 Retirees & Early Retirees

If you live off a pension, investment income, or have savings sufficient to support yourself without working in Spain, the NLV is designed for you. We specialise in retirees and FIRE / early retirees relocating to Spain.

👨‍👩‍👧 Families Relocating Together

Spouse, civil partner, and dependent children can be included in a joint family application. We manage the entire family's paperwork as a coordinated unit — see our family application service for full details and pricing.

🇬🇧 UK Citizens Post-Brexit

British nationals are among the biggest users of the NLV since Brexit removed the right to live freely in Spain. We handle UK applications through all three UK Spanish consulates — London, Manchester, and Edinburgh.

🌍 Non-EU Citizens Worldwide

We support applicants from the USA, Canada, Australia, Ireland, South Africa, and beyond. Each consulate has subtly different requirements — our immigration specialists know them all.

Costs Outside the Service Fee When You Apply for Non Lucrative Visa Spain

These are government and third-party costs you pay directly — not included in our €1,499 fee.

🏛️ Consulate Application Fee

A government visa fee payable directly to the Spanish consulate at your appointment. The exact amount varies by country and consulate — your case manager will confirm the current rate for your location.

🏥 Private Health Insurance

Spanish law requires comprehensive private health insurance with no co-payments and no excess. Premiums typically run €500–€2,000 per year depending on your age, level of cover, and location in Spain. Our partners — spanish-healthinsurance.com and 247expatinsurance.com — can find you the best rate.

📄 Criminal Record Certificate

A certified criminal record from your country of nationality, typically valid for 3 months. Your case manager advises on exactly which type and from which authority based on your consulate's requirements.

🌐 Additional Translations

Up to €100 of translations is included in your service fee. Documents exceeding this are translated at cost — your specialist will always flag this before commissioning any extra work.

🩺 Medical Certificate

A doctor's note confirming you do not have any disease that could have serious public health repercussions. Usually obtained via your GP for a small fee and translated into Spanish.

✈️ Travel to Spain

Once your visa is approved, you must enter Spain within the validity period (typically 3 months). Initial flights, accommodation, and NIE registration in Spain are separate costs.

Non Lucrative Visa Application Spain — Frequently Asked Questions

What does your NLV application service include?

Our full managed non lucrative visa application Spain service covers eligibility assessment, income and savings verification, a bespoke document checklist, full document review, sworn translations (up to €100 of translations included in your service fee), health insurance guidance via our partner insurers, consulate-ready dossier preparation, appointment briefing, and post-submission support — all tracked through your 24/7 online dashboard.

How much does the full non lucrative visa application Spain service cost?

Our service fee is €1,499 total, paid across three milestones: €400 at sign-up, €400 when your document review is complete, and €699 at dossier submission. This covers all the work our team does. Government consulate fees, health insurance premiums (typically €500–€2,000/year), and criminal record certificates are paid directly by you and are separate.

How long does the non lucrative visa application Spain process take?

Once you start the process with us, most clients have their dossier ready to submit within 6–8 weeks. The consulate then has up to 90 days to decide, though many decisions come within 4–8 weeks. Total time from sign-up to visa-in-hand is typically 3–5 months depending on consulate volumes and how quickly documents can be gathered.

Do I need to be in Spain or visit Spain during the application?

No. The entire application is managed remotely via your online dashboard until your consulate appointment — which takes place at the Spanish consulate in your home country. You do not need to travel to Spain until after your visa has been approved.

What income do I need to apply for Spain's Non-Lucrative Visa?

The Spanish government sets an income threshold linked to the IPREM. For 2026, this is approximately €2,400/month for a sole applicant, with an additional ~€600/month per additional family member. These figures are updated annually. Our eligibility assessment confirms whether your income, pension, investments, or savings meet the threshold for your specific consulate.

Can my family apply for the Spain non lucrative visa at the same time as me?

Yes. Spouses, civil partners, and dependent children can be included in a joint family application. The income threshold increases by approximately €600/month per additional family member. Each additional adult requires their own financial documentation. Ask about our family application service for bundled, coordinated management.

What happens if my non lucrative visa Spain application is refused?

If your application is refused, we review the refusal grounds and advise on the best course of action — whether that is reapplying with stronger documentation or lodging a formal appeal (recurso de alzada). A refusal does not bar you from reapplying. We offer a dedicated appeal service for rejected NLV applications. Contact us as soon as you receive a refusal letter — the appeal window is one month.

Can I work in Spain on a Non-Lucrative Visa?

No. The Non-Lucrative Visa expressly prohibits working for a Spanish employer or running a Spanish business. If you intend to work remotely for a non-Spanish employer, Spain has a separate Digital Nomad Visa for this purpose. The NLV is for those who can support themselves without any active employment income from Spain.
