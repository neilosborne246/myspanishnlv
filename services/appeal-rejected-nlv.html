Why Is Spain's Non Lucrative Visa Refused? Most Common Rejection Reasons

Understanding why applications are refused is the first step to fixing the problem. These are the grounds we see most frequently.

💰

Insufficient Income or Savings Proof

The most common reason for Spain visa rejection. Either the income threshold (~€2,400/month) was not met, or the evidence provided — bank statements, pension letters, investment documents — did not satisfy the consulate that funds were sufficient and sustainable.

📄

Documents Not Apostilled or Translated Correctly

Spain requires specific documents to carry an apostille stamp and to be translated by a sworn translator (traductor jurado). Documents that use an unofficial translator, are apostilled for the wrong country, or are presented in the wrong format are a frequent cause of Spain non lucrative visa rejection.

🏥

Non-Compliant Health Insurance Policy

Spain's NLV requires comprehensive private health insurance with no co-payments, no excess, and full coverage in Spain. Policies that include co-payments or excess — even small amounts — lead to refusals. See our health insurance requirement guide.

🔏

Criminal Record Certificate Issues

Problems include using the wrong type of certificate, certificates that have expired (typically valid for 3 months), certificates from the wrong country or authority, or certificates lacking the correct apostille or translation.

📋

Missing Documents or Forms

Each consulate has its own specific checklist. A missing document — even a minor one — can lead to a Spain visa rejection rather than a request to resubmit. Our specialists know each consulate's requirements inside out.

🏛️

Consulate-Specific Requirements Not Met

Individual Spanish consulates can apply requirements beyond the baseline national rules. What passes at one consulate may not pass at another. Failing to meet a specific consulate's local requirements is a common and frustrating cause of Spain non lucrative visa rejection.

Your Two Options After a Spain Non Lucrative Visa Rejection

After receiving a refusal, you have a choice. Our immigration specialists advise on which route gives you the best chance of success.

1

Lodge a Formal Administrative Appeal (Recurso de Alzada)

This is a formal challenge to the Spain visa rejection decision lodged with the consulate or the relevant Spanish authority. You have one month from the date of the refusal letter to submit this appeal.

An appeal is most viable when the refusal was based on a procedural error, a misapplication of the rules, or a factual mistake by the consulate. It is not the right route if the underlying application had genuine weaknesses — an appeal based on a flawed application is unlikely to succeed.

We advise whether your grounds are strong enough to appeal Spain non lucrative visa rejection based on the specific language of your refusal letter.

2

Reapply With a Strengthened Application

In most cases where the Spain non lucrative visa rejection was due to insufficient documentation, non-compliant insurance, or inadequate financial evidence, the fastest and most practical route is to reapply — this time with a correctly prepared, fully compliant dossier.

Reapplying correctly requires understanding exactly what the consulate found lacking and addressing those specific points precisely. A generic reapplication that does not fix the root cause is likely to be refused again.

We prepare your strengthened reapplication with full knowledge of your original refusal grounds.

Appeal Spain Non Lucrative Visa Rejection — Frequently Asked Questions

Can I appeal a Spain non lucrative visa rejection?

Yes. If your NLV application is refused, you have the right to lodge a formal administrative appeal — known in Spain as a recurso de alzada — within one month of the date on your refusal letter. Whether an appeal is worth pursuing depends entirely on the specific grounds for Spain non lucrative visa rejection. Our immigration specialists review your refusal letter and give you a clear, honest recommendation on whether an appeal has strong grounds or whether reapplying is the faster, more practical route.

How long do I have to appeal a Spain visa rejection?

You have one month from the date of the refusal letter to lodge a formal administrative appeal (recurso de alzada). This is a strict deadline — missing it means you lose the right to appeal that specific decision. It is critical to act quickly. Contact us as soon as you receive a Spain non lucrative visa rejection so we can review the grounds and advise within the appeal window.

Is it better to appeal or reapply after a Spain visa rejection?

It depends on why you were refused. If the refusal was based on a procedural error or a mistake by the consulate in applying the rules, an appeal may be viable. If the Spain non lucrative visa rejection was because your documents were insufficient, your health insurance was not compliant, or your financial evidence did not meet the threshold, reapplying with a strengthened application is almost always the faster and more practical route. Our immigration specialists review your specific grounds and give you a clear, honest recommendation.

Can I stay in Spain while my NLV appeal is being processed?

The NLV application is submitted from abroad at a Spanish consulate, so a Spain visa rejection at the initial application stage generally means you are outside Spain. If you were already in Spain on another basis — such as a tourist visa — when the refusal came, your right to remain depends on that separate permission, not the NLV application. We can advise on your specific situation when you contact us for your free refusal review.

Will a Spain non lucrative visa rejection affect future applications?

A refusal does not automatically bar you from reapplying for the NLV. Many applicants who receive a Spain non lucrative visa rejection go on to receive their NLV after addressing the reason for refusal and submitting a stronger, correctly prepared application. However, it is important to understand exactly why you were refused before reapplying — submitting the same flawed application again is highly unlikely to succeed. Our refusal review ensures you know precisely what went wrong and what must be fixed.

How long does a Spain NLV appeal take?

A formal administrative appeal (recurso de alzada) to appeal Spain non lucrative visa rejection is typically resolved within 3 months, though processing times vary. If the appeal is not resolved within 3 months, it is generally considered rejected by administrative silence, after which further legal options may be available. Our immigration specialists advise on realistic timelines and the best strategy for your specific situation.

How much does it cost to appeal a Spain non lucrative visa rejection?

Our initial refusal review is free — send us your Spain non lucrative visa rejection letter and we assess your grounds at no charge. Full appeal preparation or managed reapplication is quoted on enquiry depending on the complexity of your grounds and whether appeal or reapplication is the recommended route. There is no obligation to proceed after the initial free review.

How soon can I reapply after a Spain NLV rejection?

There is no mandatory waiting period before reapplying for Spain's NLV after a Spain visa rejection. However, it is essential to understand why your application was refused and to address those specific issues before reapplying. Submitting the same application again without changes is almost certain to result in another refusal. Our team helps you identify exactly what needs to be fixed and manages your strengthened reapplication.
