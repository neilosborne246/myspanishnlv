Your first Non-Lucrative Visa is granted for one year. Before it expires, you must apply to renew non lucrative visa Spain — this is not automatic and requires a fresh application with updated evidence.

How Does Spain NLV Renewal 2026 Differ From Your Original Application?

The renewal is simpler in some respects — but it has its own specific requirements that trip people up.

Fewer Documents in Many Cases

For the renewal, many of the original documents — such as your apostilled criminal record certificate — do not need to be resubmitted at the first renewal. The focus shifts to proving you have been living in Spain as intended and that you continue to meet the financial threshold.

Updated Financials Required

You must provide fresh proof that you continue to meet the income threshold. The IPREM-linked threshold is updated annually, so the figure for your renewal year may differ from what was required for your original application.

Proof of Residence in Spain

Unlike the original application — which was submitted from abroad — your renewal is made from within Spain. You must demonstrate you have been physically resident: your empadronamiento certificate and TIE card are key documents here.

Submitted to a Different Office

Your original NLV was submitted to a Spanish consulate abroad. Your renewal goes to the regional immigration office (Oficina de Extranjería) in your province of residence in Spain. Requirements can vary by region — our immigration specialists know the local nuances.

Renew Non Lucrative Visa Spain — Frequently Asked Questions

When should I start the process to renew non lucrative visa Spain?

You can apply to renew within 60 days before your permit expires, or up to 90 days after expiry (though renewing after expiry carries risk). We recommend starting the renewal process at least 3 months before your permit expires to allow enough time to gather updated documents. Do not leave it to the last minute — if your Spain NLV renewal is rejected or delayed, your legal status could be affected.

What documents do I need to renew non lucrative visa Spain?

Your Spain NLV renewal 2026 typically requires updated proof of income or savings (meeting the current year's threshold — approximately €2,400/month for a single applicant), your empadronamiento certificate, a valid passport, your current TIE card, and updated private health insurance with no co-payments and no excess. Some regional immigration offices require additional supporting documents. Your case manager will provide a complete tailored checklist.

What if my NLV has already expired — can I still renew?

If your NLV has expired, you can still apply to renew non lucrative visa Spain within 90 days after expiry. However, this is a vulnerable period — you may technically be in an irregular immigration status in Spain. If more than 90 days have passed since your permit expired, your options become significantly more limited. Contact us urgently if this applies to you — the sooner you act, the more routes remain open.

Can my whole family renew non lucrative visa Spain at the same time?

Yes. If your family members were included in your original NLV application, they will each need to renew their permits. We manage the whole family's renewal together as a coordinated unit, with one case manager handling all the paperwork and ensuring everyone's renewal is submitted correctly and simultaneously. Contact us for a family renewal quote.

How much does the Spain NLV renewal service cost?

Our full managed service to renew non lucrative visa Spain costs €699 in total, paid across two milestones: €300 at sign-up (covering eligibility re-check, document checklist, and dashboard activation) and €399 when your renewal dossier is compiled and submitted (including up to €100 of translations included in your service fee). You also pay government fees directly — your case manager will confirm the figure for your situation.

How long does the non lucrative visa renewal 2026 process take?

The renewal application is submitted to your regional immigration office (Oficina de Extranjería) in Spain. Processing times vary by region and workload, but typically range from 1–3 months. Our team will have your dossier ready to submit within 4–6 weeks of starting, depending on how quickly updated documents can be gathered. You receive an acknowledgment receipt immediately on submission, which protects your legal status while you wait for a decision.

What changes at the Spain NLV renewal compared to my original application?

The renewal is submitted in Spain at your local Oficina de Extranjería — not at a consulate abroad. You need updated financial evidence and a fresh empadronamiento, but many original documents (apostilled criminal record, medical certificate) are typically not required again at the first renewal. The income threshold may have increased since your original application as it is updated annually based on the IPREM.

Can I renew my non lucrative visa Spain from outside the country?

No. Unlike your original NLV application (which was submitted at a consulate abroad), renewals must be submitted in Spain at your local Oficina de Extranjería. This is one of the key differences between the initial application and renewal. If you are outside Spain when your permit is due to expire, contact us urgently to discuss your options — your immigration status in Spain may be affected.
